# MarcOS v1.0 — One‑Pager  
### The Marc Operating Model (Compressed Overview)

MarcOS is a dual‑mode cognitive architecture bound by a recursive meta‑loop.  
It is descriptive, not prescriptive — a formalization of patterns I have lived for decades.

The system consists of three interlocking components:

- **People Route** — long‑arc human connections and bi‑directional mirrors  
- **Quiet Games** — deep, absorbing, intrinsically motivated internal work  
- **Meta‑Cognitive Klein Loop** — the recursive engine that binds the two pillars  

Together, they form a stable triad: two modes + one binding mechanism.

---

## 1. People Route  
**The external pillar.**

A curated, evolving circuit of long‑arc relationships — colleagues, mentees, peers, and fellow travelers — revisited over time with variable cadence. These conversations provide perturbations, reframing, emotional signal, and real‑world testing of ideas.

**Functions**

- Technical sounding board  
- Emotional and personal support  
- External mirrors and perturbations  
- Long‑arc collaboration and trust  

**Why it matters**

It keeps me connected to reality and prevents my thinking from becoming too self‑consistent.

See: [`people-route.md`](people-route.md)

---

## 2. Quiet Games  
**The internal pillar.**

The deep, absorbing, intrinsically motivated pursuits I disappear into — tinkering, rebuilding, coding, restoring, designing, documenting. This is the internal laboratory where ideas are shaped, systems are built, and long‑arc projects accumulate value.

**Forms**

- Systems teardown & rebuild  
- Coding, modeling, documenting  
- Mechanical/electrical restoration  
- Research → design → build → refine  

**Why it matters**

It provides clarity, structure, and synthesis — the internal counterweight to the People Route.  
It is also where I do the internal work that strengthens how I show up for others.

See: [`quiet-games.md`](quiet-games.md)

---

## 3. Meta‑Cognitive Klein Loop  
**The binding mechanism.**

The recursive engine that integrates the two pillars.  
I think about how I think.  
I test ideas externally, refine them internally, and repeat.

**Cycle**

1. Model internally  
2. Test externally  
3. Observe what holds and what breaks  
4. Refine  
5. Repeat  

**Why it matters**

It keeps me evolving — not just doing, but improving the *way* I do.

See: [`meta-loop-explained.md`](meta-loop-explained.md)

---

## 4. The Flow‑State Email Phenomenon  
**The uncompressed output.**

When the internal recursion is ON at 100%, my communication becomes long, dense, and winding — the raw signal of MarcOS before compression. These emails are authentic, unfiltered, and — I can only hope — occasionally illuminating. They are the uncompressed version of the Operating Model.

See: [`flow-state-and-circuitous-emails.md`](flow-state-and-circuitous-emails.md)
